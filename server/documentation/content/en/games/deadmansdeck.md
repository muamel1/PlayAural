\*\*Dead Man's Deck\*\*

Dead Man's Deck is a tense bluffing card game about lying, reading the table, and surviving the revolver. You are not trying to score points. You are trying to stay alive until every other player has been eliminated.

If you are new to bluffing games, think of each turn as a hidden statement. A player places cards face down and says what those cards are. The cards may match the statement, or the player may be lying. Other players must decide whether to believe the claim or call the lie.

\*\*Goal\*\*

Be the last surviving player at the table.

You can survive in two ways:

\* Make believable claims so other players hesitate to challenge you.
\* Catch other players when they make false claims.

Losing a challenge does not simply cost points. The loser must face their own revolver. If the chamber fires, that player is eliminated from the game.

\*\*Cards\*\*

Dead Man's Deck uses a small 20-card deck:

\* 6 Aces.
\* 6 Kings.
\* 6 Queens.
\* 2 Jokers.

At the start of each round, the game chooses one target rank: Aces, Kings, or Queens. Every claim that round is supposed to be about that target rank.

Jokers are wild. A Joker always counts as truthful for the target rank.

\*\*Round Setup\*\*

Each round works like this:

\* The surviving players are put into a new random turn order.
\* A target rank is announced.
\* Each surviving player receives 5 cards.
\* Your hand is read privately to you.
\* The first player in the randomized order takes the first turn.

During the short round transition, old cards are cleared before the new hand is dealt. Outside that transition, your cards stay visible so you can keep reviewing your hand.

\*\*Your Turn\*\*

On your turn, choose 1 to 3 cards from your hand, then play the selected cards face down.

When you play them, you do not announce their real ranks. You automatically claim that all of them are the target rank.

Example: if the target rank is Kings and you play two cards, the table hears that you claimed two Kings.

Those cards might really be:

\* Two Kings, which is truthful.
\* One King and one Joker, which is also truthful because the Joker is wild.
\* A King and a Queen, which is a lie.
\* Two non-Kings, which is also a lie.

\*\*Selecting Cards\*\*

You may select at most 3 cards for a claim.

Selected cards remain visible in your hand until you play them or clear the selection. If you try to select a fourth card, the game will tell you that a claim can contain at most three cards.

You can still review your cards when it is not your turn or while a reveal, challenge, or revolver sequence is playing. If you try to select a card at the wrong time, the game will explain why the selection cannot be made yet.

\*\*Calling Liar\*\*

After a player makes a claim, the next eligible player may call liar instead of making a new claim.

Only the most recent claim can be challenged. If the next player chooses to play cards instead, the previous claim is accepted and can no longer be challenged.

When liar is called:

\* The claimed cards are revealed.
\* If every revealed card is the target rank or a Joker, the claim was truthful.
\* If any revealed card is not the target rank and not a Joker, the claim was a lie.

\*\*Challenge Results\*\*

\* \*\*Lie caught:\*\* The accused player loses the challenge.
\* \*\*Truthful claim:\*\* The challenger loses the challenge.

The loser of the challenge faces the revolver.

\*\*The Revolver\*\*

At the start of the match, each player receives a six-chamber revolver with one hidden bullet.

When a player loses a challenge, they must pull the trigger:

\* If the chamber is empty, they survive and the game continues.
\* If the gun fires, they are eliminated.

Empty chambers are used up. This means a player who survives one pull is in greater danger the next time they must pull.

\*\*Running Out Of Cards\*\*

If a player plays their last card, the table is clearly told that they have no cards left.

A player with no cards cannot make new claims. They may still be involved in the round if the rules force a challenge. If the round reaches a point where it cannot continue because too few players have cards, the next valid player is forced to challenge the last claim.

\*\*Winning\*\*

There are no points and no score target.

The game ends when only one player is still alive. That player wins the match.

\*\*Customizable Options\*\*

Dead Man's Deck currently uses fixed rules. The host has no game-specific options.

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*C:\*\* Call liar.
\* \*\*P:\*\* Play selected cards.
\* \*\*X:\*\* Clear selected cards.
\* \*\*H:\*\* Read your hand.
\* \*\*E:\*\* Read how many cards each player has.
\* \*\*V:\*\* Read the table.
\* \*\*R:\*\* Read revolver status.
\* \*\*T:\*\* Check whose turn it is.
\* \*\*Ctrl+U:\*\* Hear who is at the table.
