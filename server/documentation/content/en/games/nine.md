\*\*Nine\*\*



Nine, also known as Deviatka, is a Russian shedding card game where players race to empty their hands by building four suit sequences on the table.



\*\*Gameplay\*\*



Nine uses a 36-card deck containing ranks Six through Ace in each of the four suits. This table supports 3, 4, or 6 players so the full deck is dealt evenly.



Cards are dealt one at a time until every player has the same number of cards: 12 cards each for 3 players, 9 each for 4, and 6 each for 6.



The player holding the \*\*nine of diamonds\*\* always goes first and must open the game by playing it. After that, play proceeds in turn order around the table.



On your turn you may do one of the following:

\* \*\*Play a nine:\*\* If you hold a nine of a suit whose sequence has not yet been started, you may play it to begin that suit's sequence.

\* \*\*Extend a sequence:\*\* Play any card that sits exactly one rank lower than the current low end or one rank higher than the current high end of an existing sequence in that suit. Sequences grow outward from the nine toward Six at the low end and Ace at the high end.

\* \*\*Skip automatically:\*\* If you have no legal card to play, your turn is skipped automatically. You cannot skip while any legal play exists.



The first player to play all of their cards wins. At the end of the game, remaining players are ranked by how many cards they still hold.



\*\*Example Turn\*\*



It is Alice's turn. The diamonds sequence currently runs Eight through Ten, and no hearts sequence has been started yet. Alice holds the Seven of Diamonds, the Nine of Hearts, and the Jack of Clubs.



Alice cannot play the Jack of Clubs because there is no clubs sequence yet and it is not a nine. She can play the Seven of Diamonds to extend the diamonds sequence downward, or play the Nine of Hearts to start the hearts sequence.



She plays the Nine of Hearts, opening the hearts sequence on the table. Her turn ends and play passes to the next player.



\*\*Game Options\*\*



Nine has no game-specific options.



\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*



\* \*\*C:\*\* Check the current state of all four suit sequences on the table.

\* \*\*E:\*\* Check how many cards each player is holding.

\* \*\*T:\*\* Check whose turn it is.

\* \*\*Ctrl+U:\*\* Check who is at the table.
