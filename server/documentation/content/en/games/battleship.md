\*\*Battleship\*\*



Battleship is a classic two-player naval combat game. Each commander secretly deploys a fleet of five warships on a grid, then takes turns firing shots at the opponent's waters. The objective is to locate and sink all enemy vessels before your own fleet is destroyed.



\*\*Gameplay\*\*



A match is divided into two phases: deployment and battle. During deployment, each player positions their fleet on a square grid. Once both sides are ready, the battle begins and players alternate firing shots at enemy coordinates. The first commander to sink the entire enemy fleet wins the match.



\*\*Deployment Phase\*\*



Each player has five ships to deploy:



\* \*\*Carrier:\*\* 5 cells long.

\* \*\*Battleship:\*\* 4 cells long.

\* \*\*Destroyer:\*\* 3 cells long.

\* \*\*Submarine:\*\* 3 cells long.

\* \*\*Patrol Boat:\*\* 2 cells long.



In automatic mode, all ships are placed randomly at the start of the match and battle begins immediately.



In manual mode, you place ships one at a time. Navigate the grid to select a starting coordinate, then choose horizontal or vertical orientation. Ships cannot overlap or extend beyond the grid.



\*\*Battle Phase\*\*



Players alternate turns firing at the opponent's grid. Navigate to a coordinate on the target grid and press Enter or Space to fire. After a brief delay, the result is announced: hit or miss.



When all cells of a ship have been hit, that ship is sunk and both players are notified which vessel was destroyed. If the extra salvo on hit option is enabled, a successful hit grants an additional shot before the turn passes to the opponent.



The match ends when one player sinks all five of the opponent's ships. An end-of-match summary shows each player's total shots, hits, and accuracy percentage.



\*\*Dual Grids\*\*



During battle, each player can switch between two views:



\* \*\*Target grid:\*\* Shows where you have fired at the opponent's waters. Uncharted cells, hits, and misses are displayed.

\* \*\*Your waters:\*\* Shows your own fleet positions, any incoming hits on your ships, and missed shots by the opponent.



The target grid is shown by default during battle. Press V to toggle between views.



\*\*Customizable Options\*\*



The host can adjust the following settings before the match starts:



\* \*\*Combat Zone:\*\* The grid size. Choices: 6 by 6, 8 by 8, 10 by 10, 12 by 12. Default: 10 by 10.

\* \*\*Deployment:\*\* Choose automatic or manual ship placement. Default: Automatic.

\* \*\*Extra Salvo On Hit:\*\* When enabled, a hit grants another shot. Default: Off.

\* \*\*Turn Timer:\*\* Time limit per turn. Choices: Off, 30, 45, or 60 seconds. If the timer runs out, a shot is fired at a random coordinate. Default: Off.



\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*



\* \*\*Arrow keys:\*\* Navigate the grid.

\* \*\*Enter or Space:\*\* Fire at the selected coordinate (battle) or select a cell for ship placement (deployment).

\* \*\*V:\*\* Toggle between your waters and the target grid.

\* \*\*F:\*\* Read your fleet status: which ships are intact, damaged, or sunk.

\* \*\*E:\*\* Read enemy fleet intel: how many ships you have sunk so far.
