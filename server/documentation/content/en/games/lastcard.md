\*\*Last Card\*\*



Last Card is an Uno-style shedding card game where players race to be the first to empty their hand by matching cards by color or number. Originally popularized as a family card game in the 1970s, it became one of the most widely played card games in the world. If you have ever played Uno, Crazy Eights, or Mau-Mau, you already know the fundamentals -- Last Card builds on that same core with a wealth of customizable house rules and strategic options.



\*\*Gameplay\*\*



The game supports 2 to 10 players and uses a 108-card deck consisting of four colors (Red, Blue, Green, Yellow), numbered cards 0 through 9, and several special action cards. Each round, every player is dealt a hand of cards (7 by default), and a starting card is turned face-up to begin the discard pile. The starting card is always a number card (never a Wild or action card).



On your turn, you must play a card from your hand that matches either the color or the rank of the top card on the discard pile. For example, if the top card is a Green 5, you can play any Green card or any 5 from another color. If you cannot play any card, you must draw from the deck.



The first player to empty their hand wins the round and scores points based on the cards remaining in their opponents' hands. Rounds continue until a player reaches the winning score.



\*\*The Cards\*\*



\* \*\*Number Cards (0-9):\*\* Each color has one 0 and two each of 1 through 9. These are worth their face value in points.

\* \*\*Skip:\*\* Skips the next player's turn. Worth 20 points.

\* \*\*Reverse:\*\* Reverses the direction of play. In a two-player game, it acts as a skip by default. Worth 20 points.

\* \*\*Draw Two:\*\* Forces the next player to draw 2 cards and lose their turn (unless stacking is enabled). Worth 20 points.

\* \*\*Wild:\*\* Can be played on top of anything. When you play a Wild, you choose the color that the next player must follow. Worth 50 points.

\* \*\*Wild Draw Four:\*\* The most powerful card. Like a Wild, you choose the color, and the next player must draw 4 cards and lose their turn. Because this card is meant to be a last resort, the next player is allowed to Challenge it. If you had another card matching the active color when you played it, the challenge succeeds and you draw the 4 cards instead. Worth 50 points.



\*\*Drawing and Passing\*\*



If you have no playable cards, you draw one card from the deck. If the drawn card is playable, you may play it immediately. Otherwise, you press Pass and your turn ends.

By default, you cannot draw if you already have a playable card in your hand. If Draw Until Playable is enabled, you may keep drawing one card at a time on that turn until you find a playable card, hit the draw limit, hit the max hand size, or run out of deck. If Force Play is enabled, a playable card you draw must be played and you cannot Pass.



\*\*Scoring\*\*



Last Card offers two scoring modes:



\* \*\*Classic (default):\*\* When a player empties their hand, they score the total point value of all cards remaining in their opponents' hands. The first player to reach the winning score wins the game.

\* \*\*Negative:\*\* When a round ends, each player receives penalty points equal to the value of the cards left in their own hand. The game ends when any player exceeds the winning score, and the player with the fewest points wins.



\*\*Last Card Callout\*\*



When you play down to your last card, the game can announce it with a Last Card callout. If the Buzzer option is also enabled, that announcement opens a brief reaction window: other players can try to catch you, and you can protect yourself by pressing B right before or after playing down to one card. If someone catches you before you buzz yourself, you draw 2 penalty cards.



\*\*Challenging a Wild Draw Four\*\*



When someone plays a Wild Draw Four against you, you can Challenge it. If the challenge is successful (meaning the player who played it had a card matching the active color), the offending player draws 4 cards instead. If the challenge fails (they played it legally), you draw 6 cards (4 plus a 2-card penalty). You can also simply Accept the draw and take the 4 cards without risk.



\*\*Customizable Options\*\*



The host can configure a wide range of settings before starting the game:



\* \*\*Winning Score:\*\* The score needed to win the game. Default: 500. Range: 50 to 10,000.

\* \*\*Hand Size:\*\* The number of cards dealt to each player at the start of each round. Default: 7. Range: 3 to 15.

\* \*\*Turn Timer:\*\* An optional countdown timer for each turn. When time runs out, the game automatically plays for you. Default: Unlimited. Choices: 5, 10, 15, 20, 30, 45, 60, or 90 seconds.



\* \*\*Draw Until Playable:\*\* When enabled, you may keep pressing Draw to draw one card at a time on the same turn until you get one you can play, instead of being limited to a single draw. Default: Off.

\* \*\*Draw Limit:\*\* When Draw Until Playable is on, this caps how many cards you can draw in a single turn. Set to 0 for no limit. Default: 0. Range: 0 to 20.



\* \*\*Stacking:\*\* Controls whether Draw Two and Wild Draw Four cards can be stacked. Default: Off.

  \* \*\*Off:\*\* Draw penalties are applied immediately. The next player draws and loses their turn.

  \* \*\*Standard:\*\* Draw Two stacks on Draw Two, Wild Draw Four stacks on Wild Draw Four. The penalty accumulates and the first player who cannot stack must draw the full total.

  \* \*\*Progressive:\*\* Both Draw Two and Wild Draw Four can be stacked on each other in any combination, creating even larger draw penalties.



\* \*\*Skip Card Behavior:\*\* Default: Skip next player.

  \* \*\*Skip next player:\*\* The next player in turn order loses their turn.

  \* \*\*Skip all, play again:\*\* All other players are skipped, and you take another turn immediately.



\* \*\*Reverse in 2-Player:\*\* Controls how the Reverse card works when only two players are at the table. Default: Acts as skip.

  \* \*\*Reverse direction:\*\* Reverses the turn order (no practical effect with 2 players, but keeps it thematic).

  \* \*\*Acts as skip:\*\* The Reverse card functions like a Skip instead.



\* \*\*Zero Card Rule:\*\* Default: None.

  \* \*\*None:\*\* Zero cards have no special effect.

  \* \*\*Rotate all hands:\*\* When a 0 is played, every player passes their entire hand to the next player in the current direction of play.



\* \*\*Seven Card Rule:\*\* Default: None.

  \* \*\*None:\*\* Seven cards have no special effect.

  \* \*\*Swap hand with opponent:\*\* When a 7 is played, you choose one opponent and swap your entire hand with theirs.



\* \*\*Jump-In:\*\* When enabled, if you have the exact same card (same color and rank) as the card just played, you can jump in out of turn by pressing J. Your card is played immediately, and the turn order continues from you. Default: Off.

\* \*\*Force Play:\*\* When enabled, you are never allowed to end your turn with a playable card still sitting in your hand. If you already have a playable card, you must play one of those cards instead of choosing Pass. If you draw and the drawn card is playable, you must play it immediately. This rule does not let you choose to draw while you already have a playable card in hand. Default: Off.

\* \*\*Last Card Callout:\*\* When enabled, playing down to one card plays the Last Card announcement. If the Buzzer is also enabled, that announcement opens a brief reaction window. If the Buzzer is disabled, the announcement still plays but the turn continues immediately. Default: On.

\* \*\*Challenge Wild Draw Four:\*\* When enabled, the next player can challenge a Wild Draw Four to check if it was played legally. Default: On.



\* \*\*Multiple Card Play:\*\* When enabled, you can select and play multiple cards of the same rank in a single turn. For example, if you have two Red 5s and a Blue 5, you can select all three and play them at once. Wilds must still be played one at a time (because you need to choose a color). Default: Off.

  When multiple cards are played, their effects stack: two Skips skip two players, two Draw Twos deal a total of 4 cards, and two Reverses cancel each other out.

  To use this feature, browse your cards and press Enter to select or deselect them. Selected cards are marked with "(selected)". When you are ready, press X to confirm and play your selection.



\* \*\*Scoring Mode:\*\* Default: Classic.

  \* \*\*Classic:\*\* The round winner gains points from opponents' remaining cards. First to reach the winning score wins.

  \* \*\*Negative:\*\* All players gain points equal to their own remaining cards. When anyone exceeds the winning score, the player with the lowest total wins.



\* \*\*Buzzer:\*\* Enables or disables the interactive reaction window for the Last Card Callout feature. When OFF, the buzzer key (B on desktop) and buzzer button (on web) are completely disabled — players cannot catch opponents or protect themselves, and the Last Card announcement becomes audio-only. Default: On.

\* \*\*Reaction Timer:\*\* The duration of interrupt windows (Last Card Callout when Buzzer is enabled, Jump-In, Wild Draw Four Challenge). Default: 4 seconds. Range: 2 to 7 seconds.

\* \*\*Max Hand Size:\*\* A safety cap on the number of cards a player can hold. Drawing stops when this limit is reached. Set to 0 for unlimited. Default: 0 (unlimited). Range: 0 to 50.



\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*



\* \*\*Space:\*\* Draw a card from the deck.

\* \*\*P:\*\* Pass your turn (after drawing).

\* \*\*X:\*\* Confirm and play selected cards (Multiple Card Play mode).

\* \*\*B:\*\* Press the buzzer (Last Card Callout).

\* \*\*C:\*\* Challenge a Wild Draw Four.

\* \*\*A:\*\* Accept the draw (decline a challenge).

\* \*\*J:\*\* Jump in (when Jump-In is enabled).

\* \*\*W:\*\* Read your hand.

\* \*\*R:\*\* Read the top card on the discard pile.

\* \*\*E:\*\* Read card counts (cards held by each player and cards remaining in the deck).

\* \*\*D:\*\* Check the current draw penalty (during stacking).

\* \*\*O:\*\* Cycle hand sorting order (by color, by rank, or unsorted).

\* \*\*Shift+T:\*\* Check the turn timer.



\*\*When choosing a color after a Wild:\*\*

\* \*\*1:\*\* Red | \*\*2:\*\* Blue | \*\*3:\*\* Green | \*\*4:\*\* Yellow
