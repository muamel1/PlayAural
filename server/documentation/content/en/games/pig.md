\*\*Pig\*\*

Pig is a push-your-luck dice game for 2 to 6 players. On each turn, you repeatedly roll one die and build a temporary turn total. You may hold that total safely at any time, but rolling a 1 loses every unbanked point from the turn.

The first player or team to hold enough points to reach the target wins immediately.

\*\*Gameplay\*\*

On your turn, choose between \*\*Roll\*\* and \*\*Hold\*\*:

\* \*\*Roll:\*\* Roll the die. A result from 2 through the highest face is added to your turn total, and you may choose again.

\* \*\*Roll a 1:\*\* You lose the entire turn total, score nothing for that turn, and play passes to the next player.

\* \*\*Hold:\*\* Add the current turn total to your permanent score and end your turn safely.

Play continues around the table until a player or team holds enough points to meet or exceed the target. There is no equal-turn final round: reaching the target ends the game immediately.

\*\*Strategy\*\*

Every roll risks the full turn total. With the standard six-sided die, a common baseline is to hold at about 20 to 25 turn points, then adjust for the score:

\* Hold earlier when you are comfortably ahead.

\* Take more risk when an opponent is close to winning.

\* If your current turn total would win the game, holding secures victory immediately.

Bots consider the die's number of sides, the minimum hold, the score gap, and how many points remain to win.

\*\*Teams\*\*

In team modes, teammates share one permanent score. Each member still takes an individual turn, and any member can hold points for the team. The team wins immediately when its shared score reaches the target.

\*\*Customizable Options\*\*

\* \*\*Target Score:\*\* The total needed to win. The default is 100, with a range from 10 to 1,000.

\* \*\*Minimum Hold:\*\* The turn total required before Hold is available. The default is 0, which follows standard Pig. It may be set as high as 999, but it must remain below the target score.

\* \*\*Die Sides:\*\* The default is the standard 6-sided die. The host may choose from 4 to 20 sides. Rolling 1 always loses the turn total, so more sides reduce the chance of losing points on each roll.

\* \*\*Team Mode:\*\* Play individually or in supported team arrangements. The selected arrangement must match the number of active players.

\*\*Personal Game Options\*\*

\* \*\*Brief Announcements:\*\* Uses shorter roll, hold, bust, round, and winner messages while keeping all essential scores.

\* \*\*Confirm Risky Actions:\*\* When enabled, a high-stakes Roll must be pressed a second time within 10 seconds. This applies when the turn total reaches the die's strategic hold threshold or when holding would already win the game.

\*\*Turn Status\*\*

\*\*Check Turn Status\*\* opens a live panel showing the target, current round, active player's banked score and turn total, the score they would have after holding, and the current standings.

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*R:\*\* Roll the die.

\* \*\*H:\*\* Hold the current turn total.

\* \*\*C:\*\* Check turn status.

\* \*\*T:\*\* Check whose turn it is.

\* \*\*S:\*\* Check scores.

\* \*\*Shift+S:\*\* Open detailed scores.
